How do young people describe our new President Duterte?

Rock and roll President.

Posted by Sayet on August 24, 2016

MANILA, Philippines – President Rodrigo Duterte took his oath in Malacañang Palace on Thursday, June 30, making him the 16th president of the Philippines.

In his inaugural speech, the mayor-turned-president said that he is elected not to serve “the interests of any one person or any group or any one class. I serve every one and not only one.” He declared, “I have no friends to serve, I have no enemies to harm.”

He ended his inaugural speech, saying, “I am here because I love my country and I love the people of the Philippines. I am here, why? Because I am ready to start my work for the nation.”

On May 9, millions of Filipinos trooped to the polling precincts to cast their ballots. With a record-breaking voters’ turnout of 81%, the nation elected its next leaders. Dubbed as the first social media-led presidential elections which produced a Mindanaoan for President and a former Congresswoman for Vice President, the 2016 presidential elections highlighted the role of the youth in nation-buildng.
